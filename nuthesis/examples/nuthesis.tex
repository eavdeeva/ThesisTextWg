\begin{abstract}
The measurement of the cross section of the $W\gamma\rightarrow l\nu\gamma$ production in proton-proton collisions using 19.6~fb$^{-1}$ of LHC data collected by CMS detector at center-of-mass collision energy of $\sqrt{s}=8$~TeV is reported. We study the $W\gamma$ production because it involves a pure gauge boson coupling, a $WW\gamma$ vertex, in the Standard Model allowing us to test the electroweak sector of the Standard Model in a way not achievable by studies of other processes. In addition to the total cross section, we measure the differential cross section as a function of a photon transverse momentum. The measurement of the differential cross section is a sensitive probe for the new physics originating from the anomalous gauge coupling because possible effects of its presence increase with the photon transverse momentum and, thus, are more likely to be observed in the differential than in the total cross section. The results of this measurement agree with the Standard Model prediction at NLO in QCD, and no evidence of the anomalous triple gauge coupling has been observed.
